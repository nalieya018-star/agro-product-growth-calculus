Optimizing Oil Palm (*Elaeis guineensis*) Seedling Growth via Kinetic Modeling and Instantaneous Growth Rates.

🌱 The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysian oil palm nurseries, monitoring seedling vigor is vital for ensuring high yield potentials post-transplanting. This simulator models the average height of premium Tenera seedling varieties over a standard 24-week nursery cycle under the influence of local microbial biostimulants.

Realistic Malaysian Data Reference: Growth dynamics are adapted from standard growth curves documented by the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) and local agronomic trials evaluating seedling height parameters (0 to 90 cm baseline).

🧮 Mathematical Modeling

The growth cumulative curve $f(x)$ follows a cubic polynomial, where $x$ represents time in weeks, and $f(x)$ represents height in centimeters (cm):

Cumulative Height:
$f(x) = -0.005x^3 + 0.22x^2 + 0.8x + 5$
Instantaneous Growth Rate (Derivative):
$f'(x) = -0.015x^2 + 0.44x + 0.8$
